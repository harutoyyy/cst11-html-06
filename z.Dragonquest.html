Dragon-Quest

Dragon Quest Series (ドラゴンクエストシリーズ) is a series of Japanese computer RPGs, the first of which was Dragon Quest, released on May 27, 1986. It was created primarily by game designer Yuji Horii and released by Square Enix (formerly Enix). Abbreviated as Dracula. DRAGON QUEST" is used in the title logos of almost all the titles in the series, and the initials "DQ" are also used as an abbreviation. The title "DRAGON QUEST" was created based on a lesson Horii learned from Kazuo Koike during his time at Gekiga Murajuku: "To create an impressive title, combine easy words with difficult words," and "Changing the "ta" line to a muddled sound (da" line) makes it more memorable.
