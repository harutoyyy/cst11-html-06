Dragon-ball

A full-length manga work depicting "adventures," "dreams," "battles," and "friendship" revolving around the main character Son Goku and the Dragon Ball, a hidden treasure that can grant a single wish if all seven balls scattered around the world are collected. From 1986 to 1996, "Dragon Ball" and "Dragon Ball Z" were broadcast on Fuji Television Network, recording an average viewership rating of over 20% during the 11 years the series was on the air. The series was broadcast in more than 80 countries around the world, making it one of the most popular Japanese manga and anime series in the world. Even after the series ended, animated TV series, movies, and games have been developed, and a new animated series, "Dragon Ball DAIMA," is scheduled to begin in the fall of 2024. The number of copies of the book, including the complete edition (20 million copies), has sold more than 160 million copies in Japan and 260 million copies worldwide. More than 10 million copies of the game software have been sold so far, and the cumulative sales of the series have reached 50 million units worldwide. As of February 2023, "Dragon Ball Z: Dokkan Battle" has recorded revenues of 500 billion yen, and is a driving force behind Namco Bandai Entertainment's revenues. Super Dragon Ball Heroes" has shipped over 1 billion cards and has over 4 million registered users, making it the No. 1 most popular title in the digital kids' card game market.
